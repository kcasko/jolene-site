About Me

I'm a digital collage artist and freelance graphic designer from Pennsylvania who transforms vintage imagery into surreal, symbolic narratives.

I'm a digital collage artist and freelance graphic designer based in Pennsylvania. My work breathes fresh meaning into archival photographs and vintage illustrations. Through careful layering, color grading, and surreal juxtaposition, I create compositions that feel like visual poems—each one a doorway into an introspective, cosmic story.

Since 2015, I've built a diverse creative practice that spans editorial illustration, curated online galleries, limited-edition prints, and NFT drops.

Rooted in Pennsylvania, Active Across Platforms

Working primarily in digital media, I assemble vintage photographs, public-domain illustrations, and archival images into layered compositions that feel dreamlike, symbolic, and quietly narrative. My work invites viewers to enter an imaginative inner world where celestial elements, surreal landscapes, and figures in transformation tell stories beyond the frame.

Building a Practice Since 2015

I began freelance art and design work around 2015, and have since expanded my creative output into editorial illustration, NFT and crypto art, and online print platforms. Across my practice—whether I'm creating for clients, collectors, or personal exploration—I maintain a consistent collage-driven aesthetic that balances intuitive experimentation with careful composition.

My work has been featured across literary platforms like SmokeLong Quarterly and YNST Magazine, shared in online art communities including Reddit's r/Art, and highlighted through curated platforms like HUG.art. I'm active in online art communities and frequently exhibit new pieces through social feeds and virtual shows.

Source Materials and Techniques

My core medium is digital collage and digital illustration. I source vintage photos, archival illustrations, and public-domain imagery, then remix them into new contexts through layering, color grading, and textural unity. My technique involves assembling multiple image fragments into unified scenes, using surreal juxtapositions that feel cohesive rather than chaotic. Color and texture guide emotion and focus, turning disparate sources into singular visions.

Recurring Themes

My work often explores surreal, dreamlike environments; celestial and cosmic elements; feminine figures in states of introspection and transformation; and symbolic objects and landscapes that suggest meaning without dictating it. These themes appear across my portfolio, from editorial pieces to NFT drops to metal prints.

Artist Statement

I use digital collage to explore surreal, introspective, and cosmic narratives built from vintage and public-domain imagery. I seek to create scenes that feel like visual poems or dreams, inviting viewers to imagine stories beyond the frame. Balancing intuitive experimentation with careful composition, I use color, texture, and layering to guide emotion and focus—transforming forgotten archives into doorways for contemporary imagination.
