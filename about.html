About Jolene Casko

A digital collage artist and freelance graphic designer from Pennsylvania who transforms vintage imagery into surreal, symbolic narratives.

Rooted in Pennsylvania, Active Across Platforms

Jolene Casko is a digital collage artist and freelance graphic designer based in Pennsylvania, USA. Working primarily in digital media, she assembles vintage photographs, public-domain illustrations, and archival images into layered compositions that feel dreamlike, symbolic, and quietly narrative. Her work invites viewers to enter an imaginative inner world where celestial elements, surreal landscapes, and figures in transformation tell stories beyond the frame.

Building a Practice Since 2015

Jolene began freelance art and design work around 2015, and has since expanded her creative output into editorial illustration, NFT and crypto art, and online print platforms. Across her practice—whether she's creating for clients, collectors, or personal exploration—she maintains a consistent collage-driven aesthetic that balances intuitive experimentation with careful composition.

Her work has been featured across literary platforms like SmokeLong Quarterly and YNST Magazine, shared in online art communities including Reddit's r/Art, and highlighted through curated platforms like HUG.art. She is active in online art communities and frequently exhibits new pieces through social feeds and virtual shows.

Source Materials and Techniques

Jolene's core medium is digital collage and digital illustration. She sources vintage photos, archival illustrations, and public-domain imagery, then remixes them into new contexts through layering, color grading, and textural unity. Her technique involves assembling multiple image fragments into unified scenes, using surreal juxtapositions that feel cohesive rather than chaotic. Color and texture guide emotion and focus, turning disparate sources into singular visions.

Recurring Themes

Her work often explores surreal, dreamlike environments; celestial and cosmic elements; feminine figures in states of introspection and transformation; and symbolic objects and landscapes that suggest meaning without dictating it. These themes appear across her portfolio, from editorial pieces to NFT drops to metal prints.

Artist Statement

Jolene uses digital collage to explore surreal, introspective, and cosmic narratives built from vintage and public-domain imagery. She seeks to create scenes that feel like visual poems or dreams, inviting viewers to imagine stories beyond the frame. Balancing intuitive experimentation with careful composition, she uses color, texture, and layering to guide emotion and focus—transforming forgotten archives into doorways for contemporary imagination.
